De Broglie's Later Ground Truth (The Double Solution & Zitterbewegung):
In his original 1924 thesis, de Broglie treated the electron as perfectly at rest, yielding a strictly infinite phase velocity (vp = c²/0 = ∞). However, he later realized this was physically impossible. In his mature Theory of the Double Solution (1927), he concluded that the particle is a highly concentrated "singularity" guided by a macroscopic pilot wave.

Integrating Dirac's discoveries, de Broglie associated the electron's internal clock with Zitterbewegung (a microscopic, speed-of-light quantum jitter). Because the electron is constantly jittering at c, it is never truly at rest. The macroscopic pilot wave simply becomes a globally synchronized standing wave (pulsing all at once, rather than traveling), while the localized physical particle always has an instantaneous velocity of c. Therefore, its true local phase velocity is always exactly c (vp = c²/c = c).

Notice how the internal clock slows down to a near-halt when jittering forward at high velocities, but it never runs backward. The particle can never overtake its own superluminal wave crests! Furthermore, observe how the circular Zitterbewegung motion flattens into an ellipse in the lab frame due to Lorentz contraction.
